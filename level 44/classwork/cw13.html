Foxes are small-to-medium-sized omniv
orous mammals belonging to several
genera of the family Canidae. They have a flattened skull; upright, triangular ears; a pointed, slightly upturned snout; and a long, bushy tail ("brush").Twelve species belong to the monophyletic "true fox" group of genus Vulpes. Another 25 current or extinct species are sometimes called foxes – they are part of the paraphyletic group of the South American foxes or
an outlying group, which consists of the bat-eared fox, gray fox, and island fox.[1]
